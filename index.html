Some interesting facts about camels


  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat
  • Camels are smarter than horses

Some interesting facts about chameleons


  • Almost half of the species live in Madagascar.
  • Skin crystals enable them to change color.
  • Chameleons can't regrow their tails.

Some interesting facts about pigeons


  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities.
  • Despite the social perception as dirty and disease-ridden, pigeons are actually very clean animals and there is very little evidence to suggest that they are significant transmitters of disease.
  • Pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet, and at an average speed of 77.6 mph.

Some interesting facts about teddy bears


  • The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.
  • In 1902 in Germany, Steiff launched “Bear 55 PB”, the first toy bear with jointed arms and legs.
  • Teddy Bear was invented in honor of President Theodore Roosevelt.

Some interesting facts about pandas


  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  • Pandas go from pink to white and black.
  • An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!

Some interesting facts about the flying cicada


  • The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus.
  • Most have red-orange eyes.
  • Females lay 200 to 400 eggs in tiny holes that they make in the branches of trees and shrubs.
